Match your tested iron level to a system

Everything keys off one number. Don’t have it? A $50–$150 test is the cheapest component on this page — and the field version costs nothing: water that runs clear then turns orange in the glass is dissolved ferrous iron; water already tinted at the tap is ferric.

The two gates every iron filter must pass

Gate one: pH. Most iron media wants pH ~7.5 or higher — higher still when manganese rides along. On an acidic well, the iron filter you install will underperform until an acid neutralizer goes ahead of it — a $1,195–$1,895 fix that’s the difference between “this filter failed” and “this filter finally had a chance.” Test pH before blaming media. Gate two: pump flow. AIO backwash pulls 5–12 GPM that your well pump must sustain — a filter your pump can’t backwash is a media bed that never gets clean. Your installer should verify both numbers before anything is ordered; a quote that skips them was built from the driveway.

Frequently asked

How much does an iron filter for well water cost?

$1,000–$3,500 fully installed per HomeGuide, with Mid Atlantic Water’s 2026 guide putting the working range at $1,500–$4,500. The sweet spot for most iron wells: chemical-free AIO air-injection systems at $1,200–$2,500 installed.

What’s the best type of iron filter — AIO, greensand, or chemical injection?

For most residential wells: AIO air injection — one tank handles iron (to ~10–15 ppm by class), sulfur, and manganese with zero chemicals. Greensand needs potassium permanganate handling; chemical injection earns its complexity only above ~15 ppm or with iron bacteria.

Can a water softener remove iron instead of an iron filter?

Only trace amounts — under roughly 1 ppm of dissolved (ferrous) iron. Above that, iron fouls softener resin, killing capacity in 6–18 months and voiding most resin warranties. The iron filter isn’t an alternative to the softener; it’s the thing that keeps the softener alive.

What do iron filters cost to run each year?

AIO systems: essentially $0 in chemicals — the media lasts 6–8 years and re-beds at roughly $300 per cubic foot. Chemical-based systems (greensand, injection) run $100–$250 per year in chemicals, refills, and pump service.

Does my water’s pH matter for an iron filter?

A lot: most iron media wants pH ~7.5 or higher (higher still when manganese is present). Acidic wells need an acid neutralizer ahead of the iron filter — skipping it is why some “failed” iron filters were actually pH problems.

How do I know if my iron is ferrous or ferric?

The glass test: water that runs clear from the tap and turns orange as it sits is dissolved ferrous iron. Water already tinted at the tap is ferric (particulate). A lab test gives the exact number the sizing decision needs — $50–$150.
