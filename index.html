The short answer

Most homeowners pay $1,000–$3,000 installed for a salt-based water softener, and the national range runs $840–$4,120 once you count loop runs and labor. The unit itself is usually the smaller half of the bill — the plumbing situation in your garage decides the rest.

Frequently asked

What does a water softener cost installed?

Nationally, $840–$4,120 installed. Most homes with an existing loop land between $1,000 and $2,100. The loop is the swing factor — running one adds $600–$2,000.

Why won’t dealers give prices over the phone?

Commission-based in-home selling. The quote is built around your reaction, not a price list. That’s the gap this site exists to close.

Is a salt-free system cheaper?

Upfront, roughly comparable ($900–$3,000). Ownership is cheaper — no salt — but it conditions scale rather than removing hardness, so soap and spotting behave differently.

How much is installation labor alone?

$200–$500 for a straightforward swap on an existing loop. Cutting in a new loop is plumbing work billed at $600–$2,000 depending on run length and access.

What size softener do I need?

People × 75 gallons/day × hardness (gpg) × 7 days = weekly grains. Buy the tier at or above that number — 24k–32k covers most 3-person homes on city water.

How long does a softener last?

10–15 years for the valve head, longer for tanks. Resin degrades around year 10 on chlorinated water; a rebed costs far less than a new system.
