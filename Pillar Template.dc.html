Water Softener System Cost: Every Line Item, Itemized (2026)

By Robert Miller · Updated July 8, 2026 Data updated · Jul 2026

A water softener costs $840–$4,120 installed. I spent fifteen years writing quotes for these systems, and I can tell you the spread isn't about the equipment — a good 32k-grain unit is a $600–$1,500 item. The spread is your plumbing: whether a loop exists, where the drain is, and who's holding the wrench. This page itemizes all of it, the way I would on a worksheet.

  1. What the unit itself costs
  2. What installation labor costs

  4. What ownership costs per year

What does the softener unit itself cost?

Retail, a metered 32k-grain softener runs $600–$1,500 depending on the valve head. The valve is the machine — the tanks are commodity fiberglass and brine plastic.

Dealers selling the same class of hardware in your living room commonly quote $3,500–$8,000 for the package. The difference is sales cost, not resin. See the brand price exposés for the itemized version of that story.

What does installation labor cost?

On an existing loop, a swap is $200–$500 of plumber time — two connections, a drain line, and programming. Add $40–$120 for a bypass valve and fittings if the quote doesn't include them. Ask; the good ones itemize.

Run your own numbers

The loop run — the $2,000 wildcard

A softener loop is a pre-plumbed bypass near where the unit sits. If your house has one, skip this section and smile. If it doesn't, the plumber is cutting into your main line, and that's real work: $600–$2,000 depending on run length, slab vs. crawlspace, and drain access.

This single line item is why two neighbors with identical softeners paid $1,100 and $3,300. Get the loop priced separately on any quote — it's the honest dealer's tell.

What does ownership cost per year?

Salt runs $60–$180 a year for a typical family. Budget a resin rebed ($250–$600) around year ten on chlorinated water. Full ownership math lives in the maintenance cost guide.
