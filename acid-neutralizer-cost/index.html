The chemistry that can’t overshoot

A neutralizer is a tank of crushed limestone (calcite — ~95% calcium carbonate, NSF-certified media) that gives your water the alkaline minerals your local bedrock didn’t. Acidic water dissolves the calcite on contact; the dissolved carbonate neutralizes the acid; the water exits at ~7.0–7.5. And here’s the elegant part: calcite is self-limiting — it only dissolves while the water is acidic, then stops at neutral equilibrium. No dosing pump, no metering, no electronics, no way to overcorrect. It’s the rare water system where the failure mode “too much” is chemically impossible — which is exactly why the upflow version runs with zero moving parts for decades.

The refill routine itself is a bag of calcite ($145) poured through the fill port every 18–36 months — the published framing puts total protection around $49/month. More acidic water eats media faster: the $145–$435/yr spread is the pH spread.

The hardness trade-off, stated honestly

Calcite raises pH by adding calcium — which means it adds roughly 2–5 gpg of hardness. Two honest paths from there. Most acidic well water starts naturally soft, so many homes absorb the added hardness and never need anything more — the vendors themselves say most of their neutralizer customers never add a softener. But if your water already tests 5+ gpg, the neutralizer will push it into softener territory, and the right build is neutralizer → softener in series — matched packages run $295–$495 below separate purchases, the same package logic as the well pillar’s iron math. Sequence stays law: sediment → neutralizer → iron filter → softener → UV — the neutralizer goes early because iron media wants neutral pH to work.

Frequently asked

How much does an acid neutralizer cost?

Systems run $1,195–$1,895: non-backwashing upflow units at $1,195–$1,495 (the right pick for 90%+ of homes), backwashing at $1,695–$1,895 for iron-or-sediment wells, and a FloMag blend adding ~$200 for pH below 6.0. Install: $0–$100 DIY or $300–$800 plumbed.

What does calcite media cost per year?

$145 per 50-lb bag, with most homes using 1–3 bags yearly — a typical pH-6.5 house burns about 1.5 bags ($200–$220/yr). The media dissolving is the treatment: top off through the fill port every 18–36 months. Published maintenance range: $145–$435/yr.

Does an acid neutralizer make water hard?

Yes, by design — calcite adds roughly 2–5 gpg of hardness as it dissolves. The honest nuance: most acidic well water starts naturally soft, so many homes never end up needing a softener anyway. Already at 5+ gpg? Add one downstream; matched packages save $295–$495.

Can an acid neutralizer raise the pH too much?

No — and that’s calcite’s best feature. It’s chemically self-limiting: it only dissolves while the water is acidic and stops at neutral equilibrium (~7.0–7.5). You cannot overshoot with calcite, which is why it needs no dosing, metering, or electronics.

When do I need FloMag (Corosex) instead of plain calcite?

Below pH 6.0. FloMag corrects roughly 5× faster and blends 90/10 with calcite — never straight. Field records run to pH 4.0 and below on blends; when neutralizers disappoint, it’s almost always undersizing or wrong media, not the chemistry.

How long does an acid neutralizer system last?

15–25+ years. Upflow units have no electronics, no drain, and a valve with zero moving parts — installations from 15+ years ago commonly run on all-original equipment with nothing replaced but calcite. The tank outlives several media refills.
