What [Brand] sells

[Brand]'s residential line covers three tiers: an entry metered softener, a mid-line unit with a proprietary valve, and a flagship smart model with app monitoring. All three are conventional ion-exchange systems — resin tank, brine tank, control valve. The hardware is competent; the tiers exist mainly to give the in-home rep somewhere to walk down from. Why doesn't [Brand] publish prices?

Because the price is set in your kitchen, not at the factory. I wrote quotes in this industry for fifteen years: the in-home model prices to the buyer, not the bill of materials. The rep runs a water test, anchors high, and "finds" a discount that lands wherever your hesitation does. None of that works if the number is printed on a website.
